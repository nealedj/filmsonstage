The Royal Cinema, St. Ives

{% snippet %} Although we try to maintain our primary affinity with truly independant cinemas we're happy to lend a platform to smaller cinema chains so long as they feel quirky enough to us. The Royal is such a cinema. It is owned by Merlin cinemas which have 11 cinemas dotted mostly around Devon and Cornwall. {% endsnippet %}

Our first impression of the cinema was mixed. From the outside it looks to need renovation. However, this could easily have been a humble facade for a cinema bursting with character...

We went to see The Dark Knight Rises, a film one might normally feel is best suited to a huge screen with a sound system to blow your hair back. I can't disagree but would argue that a more intimate cinema provides a slightly alternate appeal. The Royal is in a slightly awkward position. It isn't really all that small, it has three screens. The screen we were in had a similar capacity to a small screen in a multiplex. The actual screen did look slightly small in comparison to the hall so did rather diminish the immersion.

The sound did have a few problems. My sister and her family had gone to this cinema earlier that day and found that the sound was far too quiet. I was concerned that this might affect Batman. We actually found that the sound was a reasonable volume apart from during louder segments, when the sound clipped and was limited to a volume making speech indistinguishable.

If I'm honest, the experience might have been slightly tarnished by the fact that I spilled my entire pack of Revels on the floor and judged the floor too dirty to reclaim them for consumption. I don't imagine many people would be happy to eat off of cinema floors.

A nice touch was that curtains were drawn across the screen between the trailers and the feature. This sort of thing reminds me that I'm not in another anonymous Cineworld.

Merlin cinemas play host to the Penwith Film Society in the Royal on Wednesdays for slightly more alternative films.

The cinema does have a cafe but we didn't have chance to try this.

In conclusion, I liked the Royal but it felt like it lacked the character I enjoy. The experience was mixed to position overall.
