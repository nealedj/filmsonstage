4U at Theatr Gwaun, Fishguard

A theatre at heart - but makes the perfect cinema

Our visit to Fishguard was fairly unplanned. We simply decided to find a cinema during a persistently rainy holiday in Pembrokeshire. The only options near us were The Palace in Haverfordwest and 4U in Fishguard. 4U was showing Moonrise Kingdom and we thought it sounded fun.

The first feeling we had that this cinema was something special was finding out that it is a community theatre, a charity run mainly by volunteers. We couldn't help but admire the initiative. In fact, it made us realise how much we enjoy visiting independent cinemas - we could even start writing blog entries about them!

The theatre, previously known as Theatr Gwaun, used to be run by Pembrokeshire County Council but had become a victim of their spending cuts as it was running at such a loss and was shut down. After about a year of campaigning the Friends of Theatr Gwaun were able to lease the building and re-open the theatre under its new name.

The welcome from the staff was extremely warm. We paid our Â£5 per ticket and bought some sweets (in boxes - sweets in cinemas should always be sold in boxes!). There was certainly a sense of pride amongst those working in the cinema; something we'd not expect in the local multiplex!

"You almost feels as if you're doing them a favour by buying a ticket, but when you leave you'll feel like they've done you a favour."

The actual theatre both looks and feels like a theatre. It's retained every bit of the local theatre charm, this only adds to the cinema experience. In fact, it needs to keep looking like a theatre because it is still a theatre. Regular plays, live shows and workshops are also hosted in the venue. Having the curtains open to reveal the screen at the start of the film was a wonderful touch.

As an aside, Moonrise Kingdom was fantastic. It told a sweet, warm, stylish and inventive story and couldn't been more effective at raising our spirits during an especially soggy British summer.

4U at Theatr Gwaun encapsulated much of what a quirky, independent cinema should be. The fact that it is held together by a driven, active community gives it an extra draw. You almost feels as if you're doing them a favour by buying a ticket, but when you leave you'll feel like they've done you a favour.
